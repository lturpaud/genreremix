What is a water footprint?

Your water footprint is the amount of water that you use over your life. Many times people think about this as water you use to take a shower, but this can also include water that is used to grow your food.

The amount of water it takes to grow the food you eat is part of your virtual water footprint. This is water that you don't personally flush down the drain, but which you are still responsible for, as you created the demand for this water.

Your virtual water use is often much greater than the water that you use at home. For example, according to the Water Footprint Network to make just one kilogram of beef, it requires about 15415 litres of water! The United States Geological Survey states that a shower only uses about 8 litres of water a minute. In order to use as much water as it take s to make a kilogram of beef, you could take a 32 hour long shower!
